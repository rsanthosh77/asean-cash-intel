## ASEAN Cash Management Intelligence — Week of 04 April 2026

### 🏦 Competitor Moves

- **HSBC** launched Omni Collect, a unified multi-market payments collection platform with single API integration supporting e-wallets, cards, QR codes, and bank transfers across channels. This directly competes with StanChart's collections and virtual account propositions in ASEAN with real-time analytics and multi-market scalability.

- **HSBC** launched TradePay, a fully digital trade finance solution integrated into HSBCnet that enables instant loan drawdown and supplier payments without transactional document submission. This digital-first offering with just-in-time financing and automated supplier payments directly competes with StanChart's working capital and supply chain finance solutions across ASEAN.

- **J.P. Morgan** is positioning its payments capabilities prominently in APAC, highlighting cross-currency, working capital, blockchain, and liquidity solutions. JPM is actively marketing integrated payment solutions that overlap with StanChart's core ASEAN cash management franchise.

### 🏛️ Regulatory Pulse

- **HSBC** is actively preparing clients for ISO 20022 migration with readiness handbooks and use case education, positioning itself as a thought leader in the payments standard transition. StanChart must ensure equally robust ISO 20022 client education and migration support across ASEAN to avoid losing payments clients during this critical industry transition.

- A 2026 report highlights divergent payment method preferences and regulatory approaches across Southeast Asian markets, emphasizing the need for localized payment strategies. StanChart Cash Management must tailor its payments and collections solutions by market rather than applying a one-size-fits-all ASEAN approach.

### 💡 Innovation Signals

- **Ripple** has launched Ripple Treasury with native digital asset capabilities, enabling corporate treasury teams to manage fiat and digital asset liquidity in a unified system. This represents emerging competition in treasury management from crypto-native players offering integrated digital asset liquidity solutions that traditional banks may not yet provide.

- Digital payments are projected to reach $416.60 billion by 2028 across ASEAN, up from 50% of transactions in 2023, driven by regional connectivity initiatives including Thailand. StanChart must accelerate digital payments and regional connectivity capabilities to capture share of rapidly growing ASEAN cross-border payment flows.

### ⚡ So What for StanChart

- **StanChart's Sustainable Account** allows clients to maintain intraday liquidity while their cash is referenced against sustainable projects aligned with the UN Sustainable Development Goals. RMs should actively promote this ESG-linked cash management product to corporate clients seeking to meet sustainability commitments without sacrificing liquidity.

- **HSBC's comprehensive platform strategy** (Omni Collect, TradePay, MarketSpace, Treasury APIs) sets a new competitive benchmark for integrated client experience that requires StanChart to accelerate its own digital ecosystem investments. Fragmented product offerings and inconsistent API capabilities will put StanChart at risk of client attrition to HSBC's unified platform approach.

- **ISO 20022 migration** presents both risk and opportunity as HSBC positions itself as the thought leader in client education and readiness. StanChart must immediately match or exceed HSBC's migration support capabilities across ASEAN to protect the payments client base and potentially win switchers during this transition period.
